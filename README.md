## Time series — Sales & Profit trend (2022–2025)-
Sales peaked in 2024 then declined sharply into 2025 — a reversal after years of growth.
Profit remains essentially flat throughout (the gold line barely moves), meaning rising sales did not translate to better profitability.
The widening gap between sales and profit is a cost efficiency concern — discounting or high COGS may be the culprit.
## Sales by sub-category- 
Chairs and Phones together likely account for ~30% of total revenue — classic 80/20 concentration risk.
Furniture (Chairs, Tables) sells well by volume, but Furniture often has poor margins in superstore data — cross-check profit per sub-category.
Technology items (Phones, Machines, Accessories) are high-value and worth prioritising for margin improvement.
## Sales by city & region (geographic map)-
Sales are heavily concentrated on the US East and West coasts — major metro clusters visible around New York, LA, and Chicago.
Central region (teal dots) shows sparser coverage and smaller bubble sizes, suggesting lower penetration or lower order values.
A few large bubbles (likely high-value cities like NYC, LA, Seattle) dominate — losing even one key city account could meaningfully impact revenue.
South region appears underrepresented relative to its population size — potential whitespace opportunity.
## Profit by category-
Technology -is the #1 profit driver across ALL region filters — ₹147K overall, ₹138K for Technology-only filter, ₹96K for extended period. It consistently leads profit regardless of how the data is sliced.
Office Supplies-ranks 2nd in overall profit (₹126K) but surprisingly tops Technology in the West region (₹52K vs ₹42K) — the West region has unusually strong Office Supplies performance.
Furniture-is a chronic underperformer — only ₹20K profit overall against the highest sales volume. In the East region it collapses to just ₹3K profit, near zero profitability despite meaningful sales.
## Profit vs sales scatter-
Technology (brown dot)consistently sits high and to the right — high sales AND high profit. It is the most efficient revenue-to-profit converter of the three categories.
Office Supplies (gold dot) occupies the top-left quadrant — moderate-to-high profit at lower sales volumes, meaning a strong margin rate even if absolute profit is second.
Furniture (teal dot) sits low and sometimes far-right — it achieves high sales but the profit dot barely rises. This visually confirms a discount or cost problem specific to Furniture.
When the Office Supplies category filter is selected alone (all regions), its scatter dot moves to ~₹700K–₹800K sales with ₹122K profit — a 17% margin rate, well above the blended 13%.
## Shipping mode analysis-
Standard Class dominates at 59% of all sales (₹1.30M out of ₹2.19M) — customers are strongly cost-sensitive on shipping speed.
Same Day shipping is tiny at ₹0.12M (5.6%) — this either means demand for urgency is low, or the premium is a barrier to adoption.
First Class and Second Class together (₹0.77M) represent a combined ~33% of sales — there's a mid-tier segment that values some speed. This is worth a cost-to-serve analysis to determine if it erodes margin.
## Sales by customer segment-
The Consumer segment consistently holds ~50% across every region and category filter — this stability suggests loyal, recurring individual buyers.
Corporate (~31.17%) is disproportionately important because B2B orders tend to be larger in value and less discount-sensitive — a stronger B2B push could directly improve margins.
Home Office at ~18.71% is the smallest . In the West region it reaches ~19.21%, slightly above average.
